Movie Reviews

Arrival

January 2022's Movie of the Month

A linguist works with the military to communicate with alien lifeforms after twelve mysterious spacecraft appear around the world.


Director: Denis Villenauve

Lead Actress: Amy Adams, Jeremy Renner

Year of Release: 2016

Length of the movie: 1hr 56m

Maturity Rating: 12A

Review: This film displayed the skill of an intelligent director touching upon the complex topic that is 'extraterrestrial life'. The sheer brilliance of the concept and thought provoking ideas, were beautifully captured within this movie as a whole. We as a club agree that we were captivated by this film, beginning to end. This film is not your regular alien invasion action thriller, with gore and destruction. This movie offers a more philosophical approach to how our world could react to such a unique invasion. We highly recommend this film. Overall, we would like to see this film again.

Edge Of Tomorrow

February 2022's Movie of the Month

A soldier fighting aliens gets to relive the same day over and over again, the day restarting every time he dies.


Director: Doug Liman

Lead Actors: Tom Cruise, Emily Blunt

Year of Release: 2014

Length of the movie: 1hr 53m

Maturity Rating: 12

Review: On the surface, one could assume this movie was just another Tom Cruise action packed movie that lacks plot but has a lot of cool explosions and car chases. We were sorely mistaken, this movie provided a wonderful amount of suspense and curiosity. The CGI in the movie was executed well, the script was well thought out. The reason we have not rated this movie any higher is because the final moments of the movie show to be a bit ridiculous. However, we appreciate that this is fiction and still thought it was a satisfactory way to bring the film to an end.

Ex Machina

March 2022's Movie of the Month

A young programmer is selected to participate in a ground-breaking experiment in synthetic intelligence by evaluating the human qualities of a highly advanced humanoid A.I.


Director: Alex Garland

Lead Actors: Alicia Vikander, Domhnall Gleeson

Year of Release:2014

Length of the movie:1hr 48m

Maturity Rating: 15

Review: Ex Machina is a great film and story, despite the final third of the movie highlighting some plot holes. Generally the film achieves what is set out to accomplish. Each scene brings tension, leading to a climatic finally. The acting was sublime and despite being a film of technology, which would typically invite a more logical thought process; the movie has a very artistic beauty layered into it. The overall message of the film is spot on as humanity questions are relationship with AI advancements.

The Martian

April 2022's Movie of the Month

An astronaut becomes stranded on Mars after his team assume him dead, and must rely on his ingenuity to find a way to signal to Earth that he is alive and can survive until a potential rescue.


Director: Ridley Scott

Lead Actors: Matt Damon, Jessica Chastain

Year of Release: 2015

Length of the movie: 2hr 24m

Maturity Rating: 12

Review: Director Ridley Scott makes the most of a superb script, as one astronaught is stranded on the big red planet, Mars. The film beautifully brings Andy Weir's 2011 novel to life on the big screen. It has a wonderful realism as it has been based on theoretical science given humans have not yet ventured mars terrain. The film was a very good attempt at condensing the book, however the reason we have not given this film the full five stars is because the film did not capture the humour and lonliness of the lead character as well as it could have. The book provided a great internal monologue whereas the film lacked this a restricted the audience to limited emotions and consequently stunting the complexity of Matt Damon's character compared to the book. Nonetheless, the overall message of this story is so heart-warming and Matt Damon certainly has you routing for him to succeed and survive.

Gravity

May 2022's Movie of the Month

Two astronauts work together to survive after an accident leaves them stranded in space.


Director: Alfonso Cuarón

Lead Actor: Sandra Bullock, George Clooney

Year of Release: 2013

Length of the movie: 1hr 31m

Maturity Rating: 12

Review: Firstly, the visual effects for this movie are outstanding, it is one of the main factors that carries this film. Secondly, the acting was brilliant; Sandra Bullock and George Clooney really brought to life these characters with great intelligence and charisma. However, as a club we really felt the plot was lacking. We found it to be a bit boring and slightly predictable. When you compare the plot of similar space movies like The Martian or Interstellar, they have so much more to bring to the table than this movie did.

Armageddon

June 2022's Movie of the Month

After discovering that an asteroid the size of Texas will impact Earth in less than a month, NASA recruits a misfit team of deep-core drillers to save the planet.


Director: Michael Bay

Lead Actor: Bruce Willis, Ben Affleck, Liv Tyler

Year of Release: 1998

Length of the movie: 2hr 31m

Maturity Rating: 15

Review: For pure entertainment, this is a great space-themed sci-fi movie. We can forgive the special effects on the movie given that the film was released in 1998. The acting at times could be a little ham but for nostalgia purposed the club holds this film close to their hearts. The plot is ridiculos but incredibly entertaining and some aspects to it could very well happen to Earth one day. The story is intense and action pact but with a sprinkle of stupidity added into the concoction.

Interstellar

July 2022's Movie of the Month

A team of explorers travel through a wormhole in space in an attempt to ensure humanity's survival.


Director: Christopher Nolan

Lead Actor: Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway, Jessica Chastain

Year of Release: 2014

Length of the movie: 2hr 49m

Maturity Rating: 12

Review: An overwhelming majority of the club felt that this has become their favourite space-themed sci-fi film to date. Time and space are two crucial phenomenons that keeps humanity curious about our infinate universe. The highly talented Christopher Nolan demonstates his incredible directorial skills to generate this marvel of a movie. Interstellar is a ground-breaking movie, showing the hardships humans endure to try and find a new home after Earth has been disease ridden; consequently limiting food supplies. The acting was impecicable and truly immersed you into the experience. The emotion of the movie was driven by Hans Zimmer's impactful sound. Not many movies have touched on black holes, due to their mystery yet Christopher Nolan gave this a shot and did a great job and ultimatly gave a mind-blowing experience to the viewer. A full 5 stars from all the members of 'Beam Me Up'.
